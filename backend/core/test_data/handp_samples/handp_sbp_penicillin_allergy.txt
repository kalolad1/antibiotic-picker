Assessment and Plan:
Mrs. Smith presents with clinical symptoms and laboratory findings suggestive of Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP). The diagnosis is supported by the elevated ascitic fluid WBC count with a neutrophilic predominance.

Treatment:

Mrs. Smith will be started on empiric antibiotic therapy with intravenous ceftriaxone while awaiting blood culture results.
She will also receive fluid and electrolyte management to address her ascites.
Consultation with a gastroenterologist and an infectious disease specialist is recommended for further management and evaluation of her cirrhosis.
Mrs. Smith will be closely monitored during her hospital stay, with repeat ascitic fluid analysis and appropriate adjustments to her treatment plan based on culture results.
